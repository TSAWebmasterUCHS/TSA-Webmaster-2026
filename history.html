History of Blairsville, Georgia

Early Settlement

Blairsville, Georgia, was established in 1835 after Union County was created during the Georgia Gold Rush of the early 1830s. Before European American settlement, the area was home to the Cherokee Nation until they were removed during the Trail of Tears. After the land was opened through a lottery system, farmers and gold seekers settled in the region, building homes, churches, and businesses. Blairsville became the county seat and grew into an important mountain community centered on agriculture and local government.

Historic Landmarks

Blairsville is home to several historic landmarks that reflect its mountain heritage. The Union County Courthouse, built in 1899, stands in the center of town as a symbol of early growth. The Byron Herbert Reece Farm and Heritage Center preserves the home of Georgia poet Byron Herbert Reece and celebrates Appalachian culture. Historic churches, cemeteries, and buildings from the late 1800s offer visitors a glimpse into the past.

In recent years, Blairsville has experienced steady growth while maintaining its small-town charm. New businesses and tourism opportunities have strengthened the local economy.
